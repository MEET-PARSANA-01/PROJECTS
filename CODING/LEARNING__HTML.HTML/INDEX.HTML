The Rolls-Royce Phantom is a luxury car known for its quality, size, and driving experience. The Phantom is a flagship model for Rolls-Royce Motor Cars and has been in production since 2003. Here are some details about the Rolls-Royce Phantom: DESIGN: The Phantom has a Shooting Star Headliner, which is a leather roof lining with hundreds of fiber-optic lights. The interior also features open-pore Obsidian Ayous Veneers and Scivaro Grey and Peony Pink leather. FEATURES: The Phantom has a stereo camera that scans the road ahead and preconfigures the spring and damper rates, and the anti-roll bars. It also has four-wheel steering, which turns the rear wheels counter to the front wheels at speeds lower than 60 km/h. ENGINE: The Phantom has a 6.75-liter, V12 engine, which is considered to be one of the smoothest engines ever built. HISTORY: The Phantom was introduced in 1925 as the New Phantom and replaced the original Silver Ghost. The Phantom was built in Derby, England, and Springfield, Massachusetts. PRODUCTION: Production of the Phantom VII ceased in January 2017, and the Phantom VIII was launched later that year.
